The only thing that turns me off about them is C#'s implementation syntax: class SomeClassName: SomeParentClassName, ISomeInterfaceName For you see, the only way you can distinguish the difference between the interfaces and the inherited class (if there is one) is by seeing the capital 'I' in front of the interface's name.
